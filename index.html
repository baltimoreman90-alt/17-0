How to Play

How to Play 17–0

Draft a full 14-man starting lineup of NFL legends across every era and chase the only record that matters: a perfect 17–0 season and a Lombardi.

1 · The Starting Lineup

You fill a complete lineup — never two players at the same spot beyond what a real roster carries:

Offense 1 QB · 1 RB · 3 WR · 1 TE · 2 OL Defense 2 DL · 1 LB · 2 CB · 1 S

2 · The Draft Board

Each pick locks to the next position in your lineup, and the board spins to a random era — the 1960s through the 2020s. You choose the best available legend at that position from that decade. Eras can repeat across your roster; the same player can't.

3 · The Five Phases

There is no single box score in football, so every player is rated across the five phases that win games:

OFF OffenseDEF DefenseTRN TrenchesPLY PlaymakingIQ Football IQ

Your team total is the sum of all 14 starters in each phase. Linemen own the trenches, corners and safeties drive playmaking and defense — a lineup that ignores a phase gets exposed all season.

4 · The Season Curve

Your totals run through a non-linear win projection. Wins get exponentially harder near the top, and your weakest phase caps your ceiling no matter how elite the rest of the team is. To go 17–0 you must draft the best available legend nearly every pick and stay elite in everything.

5 · Era Re-spins

You get two era re-spins per game. Save them for a position where the decade you landed on is thin and a different era would hand you a stronger legend.

6 · The Playoffs

Finish 9–8 or better and you're in the postseason. Win 14+ and you take the #1 seed and a first-round bye. Each January game gets a little tougher, and your roster strength sets your odds. Survive every round to lift the Lombardi Trophy — and a 17–0 team that runs the table goes a perfect 20–0.
